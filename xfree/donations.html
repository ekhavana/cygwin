Last updated: 2002-10-01 2145 EDT

Reasons for Donations

There are a couple reasons why you may be at this page:

  1. You appreciate the features that Cygwin/XFree86 already has and you want to show your appreciation for the effort that was put into developing those features.
  2. You want to take some of the monetary burden of ensuring that my development machines are up-to-date, thus making it a *lot* easier for us to devote time to Cygwin/XFree86 development.
  3. You are an individual or business user interested in the development of a particular feature that you would find beneficial, but that no one has spent much time working on to date. Such features typically require weeks or months to develop and monetary support surely makes it easier to finish these features in a more timely manner.
  4. You are business user contributing a fair amount of money for the potentially large benefit (and cost savings) that your company derives from Cygwin/XFree86 development. You know that $500 means nothing to your project's or department's budget, but it means a complete overhaul of one of our Cygwin/XFree86 development machines.
  5. Or, you just have some money to burn :)

How the Monetary Donations are Collected

I have setup a PayPal account (links below) that can accept payments from both PayPal members and from people without PayPal accounts. You can pay with a credit card (Visa, MasterCard, Discover/Novus, and American Express) regardless of whether or not you have a PayPal account. Additionally, PayPal members may transfer funds from their PayPal account to my account; direct transfers from a PayPal account have lower fees than credit card payments, however, credit card payments are really easy so the fee (~3%) is worth it.

Cygwin/XFree86 - Project-Level Monetary Donations

We can accept, as of 2002-10-01, monetary donations for past Cygwin/XFree86 development. We will be putting up a chart later on with bids for the development of various features that many people would like but that require too much time for us to develop without some support. If you are interested in having a bid for a particular feature, feel free to email cygwin-xfree@cygwin.com with a description of the feature and we will put up a bid for you to look at. (Note: This does not say that Cygwin/XFree86 development will only occur when there is money donated; rather, this is saying that some features are so large that the chances are pretty slim of having them finished in a timely fashion without monetary support.)

To make a general project-level donation to Cygwin/XFree86 (to be used for development hardware, books, etc. for any developer that expresses a need), click the PayPal link below. Our project leader, Harold Hunt, will collect these payments and distribute them to developers as needed.
