Who are we?

Cygwin was initially developed by Cygnus Solutions (Now Red Hat, Inc.) and the project is still run by two employees from Red Hat, even though there is very little further ongoing Cygwin development within Red Hat.

Christopher Faylor (cgf) is one of the project leads and has led the project through all of its recent innovations as well as acting as the primary developer responsible for pathname and signal handling in the Cygwin DLL. Chris works at TimeSys but most of his Cygwin activities occur on his own time.

Corinna Vinschen is the other project lead. Corinna is a senior Red Hat engineer who provides substantial Cygwin support, also in her spare time. Corinna is responsible for such important subsystems as security and networking.

Harold L Hunt II was the lead for the Cygwin/X project. Harold was the driving force who has made Cygwin/X a fully functional, robust implementation of X for Windows.

Cygwin/X is currently led by Alexander Gottwald who also manages the Win32-X11 project.

Like many free software projects on sources.redhat.com, contributions from net people are especially important. Cygwin benefits from thousands of testers and dozens of contributors, including some incredible people like Sergey Okhapkin, Egor Duda, Chuck Wilson, David Starks-Browning, Kazuhiro Fujieda, Jason Tishler, Robert Collins, David Billinghurst, Joshua Daniel Franklin, Chris January, Conrad Scott, Pavel Tsekov, Pierre Humblet, Igor Pechtchanski, Daniel Reed, Thomas Pfaff, and Brian Ford.

The Engineering Services branch of Red Hat, Inc. is a leader in Open Source-based software development tools, mission critical support, and custom engineering for the embedded systems market. Cygwin is offered commercially as one of the offerings in the GNUPro Toolkit; development tools for 125 desktop and cross-platform development environments.
