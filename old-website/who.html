Who are we?

Christopher Faylor (cgf) is one of the project leads and has led the project through all of its recent innovations as well as acting as the primary developer responsible for pathname and signal handling in the Cygwin DLL. Chris works at TimeSys but most of his Cygwin activities occur on his own time.

Corinna Vinschen (corinna) is the other project lead. Corinna is a senior Red Hat engineer who provides substantial Cygwin support, also in her spare time. Corinna is responsible for such important subsystems as security and networking.

Cygwin/X is currently led by Alan Hourihane.

The Cygwin setup project is currently led by Brian Dessent with notable contributions from Igor Pechtchanski and Dave Korn.

Cygwin was initially developed by Cygnus Solutions (Now Red Hat, Inc.) but there is little further ongoing Cygwin development within Red Hat.

Please note that all of the above project leads prefer communicating on the mailing list over private email.

Like many free software projects on sourceware.org, contributions from net people are especially important. Cygwin benefits from thousands of testers and dozens of contributors, including some incredible people like Sergey Okhapkin, Egor Duda, Chuck Wilson, David Starks-Browning, Kazuhiro Fujieda, Jason Tishler, Robert Collins, David Billinghurst, Joshua Daniel Franklin, Chris January, Conrad Scott, Pavel Tsekov, Pierre Humblet, Igor Pechtchanski, Brian Dessent, Dave Korn, and Brian Ford.
