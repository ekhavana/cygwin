Who are we?

The Cygwin group at Red Hat, Inc. consists primarily of two people: Christopher Faylor (manager) and Corinna Vinschen (senior engineer).

Cygwin was originally designed and implemented by Steve Chamberlain when he worked for Cygnus.

Geoffrey Noer led Cygwin development at Cygnus for almost three years from 1996 - 1999.

Chris Faylor is the current project lead and has led the project through all of its recent innovations as well as acting as the primary developer responsible for pathname and signal handling in the Cygwin DLL.

Like many free software projects on sources.redhat.com, contributions from net people are especially important. Cygwin benefits from thousands of testers and dozens of contributors, including some incredible people like Egor Duda, Chuck Wilson, David Starks-Browning, Kazuhiro Fujieda, Jason Tishler, Robert Collins, David Billinghurst, Joshua Daniel Franklin, Chris January, and Conrad Scott. (If you know of other contributors who you think should be listed here, please let us know)

The Engineering Services branch of Red Hat, Inc. is a leader in Open Source-based software development tools, mission critical support, and custom engineering for the embedded systems market. Cygwin is offered commercially as one of the offerings in

  • the GNUPro Toolkit, development tools for 125 desktop and cross-platform development environments.
