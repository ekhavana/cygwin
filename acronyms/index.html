One encounters all sorts of acronyms on the Cygwin mailing lists. Some are well-known (such as IMHO and FAQ), others less so (such as HTH). Some were even invented on this list, and are so fresh, they haven't even been cached by Google yet. This document should present most acronyms you're likely to find on the Cygwin mailing list, with an explanation and a brief history for some. If you can't find an acronym you're looking for here, try The Canonical Acronym List or AcronymFinder.com.

So, without further ado, let me present

The OLOCA

AFAIC As Far As I'm Concerned
AFAIK As Far As I Know
AFAIR As Far As I Recall
BSOD Blue Screen Of Death. Not Cygwin's fault!
BTW By The Way
CKOA Cygwin Keeper of Acronyms (yours truly). Coined by Christopher Faylor in http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-02/msg02337.html
FAQ Frequently Asked Questions. When written as "The FAQ", refers to the Cygwin FAQ
FWIW For What It's Worth
FYI For Your Information
HTH Hope This Helps
IIRC If I Recall Correctly
IMHO In My Humble Opinion
IMNSHO In My Not So Humble Opinion. Usually reserved for people who actually know what they are doing
IMO In My Opinion
OLOCA Official List of Cygwin Acronyms. Coined by yours truly in http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-02/msg02346.html. Guess we're stuck with it now...
PPAST Please Provide a Simple Testcase. Introduced by yours truly in http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-02/msg02330.html. Don't think this one will ever take off, though... **Sigh**...
RTFM Read The Manual. Used to gently guide a newbie user to the manual page for the tool he's trying to use. The "F" is historical, and was initially added for emphasis. Nowadays it's just plain necessary.
TPIBA The Problem is Being Addressed. Kind of like "The check is in the mail", but more honest, or at least verifiable. Introduced by Christopher Faylor in http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-03/msg00009.html.
UG User's Guide. Most often refers to the Cygwin User's Guide.
WAG Wild-Assed Guess. Usually correct for certain people
WCTYBTWHTKY We Could Tell You, But Then We'd Have To Kill You. Frankly, I have yet to see this one used. But it will be a sure indicator of reaching the whole new level of meanness
YA Yet Another or Yet Again, depending on its position in the sentense
YMMV Your Mileage May Vary. Basically means "Works for me" or "Try at your own risk"
YOWTWYWT You Obviously Weren't Thinking When You Wrote That. Pronounced "Yow-twit". Applies a lot on this list. Coined by Max Bowher in http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-02/msg02119.html. Pronounciation suggested by Randall R. Schulz (http://cygwin.com/ml/cygwin/2003-02/msg02127.html) and hasn't been disputed yet
This document is work in progress. I will add more acronyms as I encounter them on the list. If you see an acronym not listed here on the list, please let me know via e-mail.
