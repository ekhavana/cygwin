Who are we?

Cygwin was initially developed by Cygnus Solutions (Now Red Hat, Inc.) and the project is still run by two employees from Red Hat, even though there is very little further ongoing Cygwin development within Red Hat.

Chris Faylor is the current project lead and has led the project through all of its recent innovations as well as acting as the primary developer responsible for pathname and signal handling in the Cygwin DLL. Although he works at Red Hat, most of his Cygwin activities occur on his own time.

Corinna Vinschen is the other senior Red Hat engineer who provides substantial Cygwin support, also in her spare time. Corinna is responsible for such important subsystems as security and networking.

Like many free software projects on sources.redhat.com, contributions from net people are especially important. Cygwin benefits from thousands of testers and dozens of contributors, including some incredible people like Egor Duda, Chuck Wilson, David Starks-Browning, Kazuhiro Fujieda, Jason Tishler, Robert Collins, David Billinghurst, Joshua Daniel Franklin, Chris January, Conrad Scott, Pavel Tsekov, and Pierre Humblet.

The Engineering Services branch of Red Hat, Inc. is a leader in Open Source-based software development tools, mission critical support, and custom engineering for the embedded systems market. Cygwin is offered commercially as one of the offerings in the GNUPro Toolkit; development tools for 125 desktop and cross-platform development environments.
